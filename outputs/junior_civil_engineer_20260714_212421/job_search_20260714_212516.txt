## 3. Market Insights

### Common Skills Across Listings
- **Technical design software:** Civil/highway design tools (e.g., AutoCAD Civil 3D or equivalent) appear repeatedly
- **Core civil engineering fundamentals:** Grading, drainage, utilities, site layout
- **Infrastructure focus:** Strong emphasis on transportation/roadway, land development, and transit projects in the NYC market
- **Soft skills:** Collaboration with multi-disciplinary teams, technical documentation, client/municipal interaction (at senior levels)
- **Licensure trajectory:** PE license is a clear differentiator between junior/entry roles and senior/leadership roles

### Experience Level Patterns
- **True entry-level postings are rare** under the "Junior" title specifically, but staffing firms like Actalent actively recruit "Entry-Level to Early Career" engineers under broader "Civil Engineer" titles
- **Clear career ladder visible:** Entry-level (0-2 yrs) → Mid-level design engineer (2-5 yrs) → PE-licensed team lead (5+ yrs)
- Junior engineers are frequently expected to work under mentorship of PE-licensed seniors, especially in land development and bridge/structural work

### Salary Trends
- **Entry-level/Junior:** $57,000 – $100,000
- **Mid-level (highway/roadway design):** $79,000 – $103,000 (location within NYC boroughs affects range — Manhattan roles trend higher)
- **Senior/PE-licensed:** $99,000 – $140,000+
- **Specialized senior (AI Data Centers, SME roles):** Up to $186,000 — indicates high-growth niche sectors command premium pay even relative to standard civil roles

### Notable Companies Hiring
- **Actalent** — Staffing/recruiting firm with the highest volume of active civil engineering postings across all experience levels in NY; strong entry-level pipeline
- **Systra** — Global rail transit leader headquartered in NYC, offering junior structural/civil roles with strong growth potential
- **Parsons Corporation** — Major infrastructure firm hiring at the Associate Engineer level with construction management exposure
- **Jacobs** — Large engineering firm expanding rapidly into AI data center infrastructure, indicating emerging niche opportunities beyond traditional civil engineering
- **RightWorks** — Specialized recruiting for structural/load rating engineering (bridge-focused), relevant for candidates interested in structural specialization

### Key Takeaway for Candidates
Junior civil engineers targeting NYC should focus applications through staffing/recruiting firms like Actalent, which explicitly market entry-level roles, while also monitoring large infrastructure firms (Systra, Parsons, Jacobs) for structured graduate programs. Building proficiency in civil design software and gaining exposure to roadway/highway or transit infrastructure projects will be critical for competitiveness, with PE licensure being the key long-term milestone for salary growth.
