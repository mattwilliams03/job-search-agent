## 3. Learning Roadmap

### 🔧 Skill #1: AutoCAD Civil 3D
- **Priority:** Critical | **Learning Time:** 2–3 months (beginner → job-ready intermediate)
- **Courses:**
  - LinkedIn Learning: *"AutoCAD Civil 3D Essential Training"* (great structured intro)
  - Udemy: *"AutoCAD Civil 3D 2024: Complete Guide from Beginner to Pro"*
  - Autodesk's own free tutorials (Autodesk Certified User curriculum)
- **Books:** *"Mastering AutoCAD Civil 3D"* by Fawad Mahdavi
- **Certification:** Autodesk Certified User (ACU) in Civil 3D — achievable in 4-6 weeks
- **Hands-on Project:** Model a small roadway segment including grading plan, drainage layout, and a cross-section profile for a fictional 500-ft residential street — post to a portfolio site or GitHub Pages with annotated screenshots

### 🔧 Skill #2: Grading, Drainage & Utility Design Fundamentals
- **Priority:** Critical | **Learning Time:** 3-4 months (parallel with software learning)
- **Courses:**
  - Coursera: *"Introduction to Civil Engineering"* (Rice University or similar)
  - YouTube: "Civil Engineering Academy" channel — excellent free drainage/grading tutorials
  - PDH Academy or PE Institute short courses on stormwater management basics
- **Books:** *"Site Engineering for Landscape Architects"* by Strom, Nathan, Woland (widely used, very practical for grading/drainage)
- **Project:** Design a stormwater management plan for a small parking lot (calculate runoff, size a detention basin) — a classic portfolio piece hiring managers recognize immediately

### 🔧 Skill #3: Roadway/Highway Design Fundamentals
- **Priority:** Critical | **Learning Time:** 3-6 months
- **Courses:** Udemy *"Highway Engineering and Design"*; NHI (National Highway Institute) free web-based training modules (nhi.fhwa.dot.gov) — highly respected, government-backed
- **Reference Standard:** Familiarize with AASHTO's *"A Policy on Geometric Design of Highways and Streets"* (the "Green Book") — even skimming key chapters signals credibility in interviews
- **Project:** Redesign a local intersection you find frustrating — sketch geometric improvements, note ADA/accessibility considerations

### 📝 Skill #4: Technical Documentation & Contract Docs
- **Priority:** Important | **Learning Time:** 4-6 weeks
- **Resources:** Coursera *"Technical Writing for Engineers"* (UC San Diego); review sample specifications from NYSDOT or NYCDOT (publicly available standard specs)
- **Project:** Write a mock technical memo summarizing your stormwater project above, formatted as if submitting to a municipal review board

### 🤝 Skill #5: Multi-disciplinary Collaboration & Coachability
- **Priority:** Important/Critical (behavioral) | **Learning Time:** Ongoing, but interview-ready in 2-4 weeks
- **Resources:** LinkedIn Learning *"Collaborating Across Teams"*; practice via student org, hackathon, or volunteer engineering project (Engineers Without Borders chapters are excellent for this)
- **Quick Win:** Prepare 2-3 STAR-format stories about working on a team project (school, internship, or volunteer) to use in behavioral interviews

### 🎓 Long-Term: FE → EIT → PE Pathway
- **Priority:** Long-term Critical | **Timeline:** FE exam within 6-12 months of graduation; PE eligible after 4 years of experience
- **Resources:** *"PPI FE Civil Review Manual"* by Michael R. Lindeburg (the industry-standard prep book); School of PE or PPI2Pass review courses
- **Action:** If not yet taken, schedule the FE exam as soon as possible — passing it BEFORE applying makes you dramatically more competitive for every listing reviewed here

## 4. Quick Start Plan (Next 30 Days)

**Top 3 Skills to Focus On Immediately:**

1. **AutoCAD Civil 3D — Week 1-4**
   - Day 1: Download free student version from Autodesk Education
   - Days 2-7: Complete LinkedIn Learning Essential Training course
   - Days 8-21: Follow along with Udemy comprehensive course, building the roadway segment project
   - Days 22-30: Polish and document your first portfolio project

2. **Grading & Drainage Fundamentals — Week 1-4 (parallel track)**
   - Day 1-3: Watch 5-6 Civil Engineering Academy YouTube videos on grading basics
   - Days 4-14: Read core chapters of *Site Engineering for Landscape Architects*
   - Days 15-30: Start sketching your stormwater management portfolio project by hand before modeling it in Civil 3D

3. **FE Exam Preparation Kickoff — Week 1-4**
   - Day 1: Register for the FE Civil exam (check NCEES.org for testing dates)
   - Days 2-30: Begin working through PPI FE Civil Review Manual, 1 chapter/week — even partial progress now compounds significantly

**Resources to start TODAY (free/immediate):**
- Autodesk Civil 3D free student license: autodesk.com/education
- NHI free highway design modules: nhi.fhwa.dot.gov
- NCEES FE exam registration: ncees.org
