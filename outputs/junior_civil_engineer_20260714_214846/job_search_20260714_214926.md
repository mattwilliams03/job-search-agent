## 3. Market Insights

### Common Skills Across Listings
- **BS in Civil Engineering** is a baseline requirement across nearly all postings.
- **PE (Professional Engineer) License** appears as a critical qualification for mid/senior roles — junior candidates should prioritize working toward licensure (FE exam → PE exam) early in their careers.
- **Structural engineering knowledge** (bridges, transit infrastructure) is highly valued in the NY market specifically, given the region's aging infrastructure and transit-focused projects.
- **Project management and municipal/regulatory experience** becomes increasingly important as engineers progress beyond entry-level.

### Experience Level Patterns
- **True junior/entry-level roles:** Only 1 of 4 listings (Systra) is explicitly positioned for junior candidates.
- **Mid-level roles (5+ years):** Load Rating Engineer position requires 5+ years and PE licensure.
- **Senior/SME roles:** Jacobs and Actalent listings are clearly senior-level, requiring leadership and licensure.
- **Pattern:** The New York civil engineering market currently has more demand for licensed, experienced engineers than for entry-level talent — junior candidates may need to cast a wider net (adjacent titles, internships, or firms with formal training programs like Systra).

### Salary Trends
- **Entry/Junior level (Systra):** ~$99,837
- **Mid-level (Load Rating Engineer):** ~$85,292 (lower than expected for experience level; may reflect base pay before licensure premiums or benefits)
- **Senior/PE-licensed roles (Actalent):** ~$99,461
- **SME/Specialized senior roles (Jacobs):** ~$186,104 (significantly higher, reflecting specialized data center infrastructure expertise)
- **Takeaway:** Salary scales sharply upward with specialization (e.g., AI/data center infrastructure) rather than just tenure — junior engineers should consider building expertise in emerging infrastructure niches for long-term earning potential.

### Notable Companies Hiring
- **Systra** — Global rail transit leader, strong entry-level pipeline and career development structure.
- **Jacobs** — Major engineering firm expanding aggressively into AI/data center infrastructure; excellent long-term target employer.
- **Actalent** — Engineering staffing/project firm with land development focus; offers mentorship-rich environment.
- **RightWorks** — Specialized in structural/bridge engineering staffing, relevant for engineers interested in transportation infrastructure.

### Recommendations for Junior Candidates
1. **Target large infrastructure firms** like Systra that have established junior engineer tracks and mentorship programs.
2. **Begin PE licensure process early** (FE exam) since nearly all growth roles require it.
3. **Consider specializing** in high-growth niches such as data center/hyperscale infrastructure (Jacobs) or transit/bridge engineering, as these show the strongest salary premiums.
4. **Expand search terms** beyond "Junior Civil Engineer" — try "Engineer I," "Associate Civil Engineer," or "Staff Engineer" to capture more entry-level opportunities in the NY market.
