## SECTION 3: DETAILED LEARNING ROADMAP

### 🔴 CRITICAL SKILL #1: AutoCAD Civil 3D
- **Learning Time:** 2-3 months for working proficiency (beginner → intermediate)
- **Courses:**
  - *LinkedIn Learning:* "AutoCAD Civil 3D Essential Training" (Steve Wilson) — ~8 hours, excellent for absolute beginners
  - *Udemy:* "AutoCAD Civil 3D 2024: Complete Guide for Beginners" — project-based, ~15 hours
  - *Autodesk Official:* Free Civil 3D tutorials via Autodesk Learning Portal
- **Books:** "Mastering AutoCAD Civil 3D" (Sybex/Autodesk Official Press series)
- **Certification:** **Autodesk Certified Professional: Civil 3D** — highly recognized by NYC employers, directly addresses Job 3/4 requirements
- **Hands-On Project:** Design a mock roadway corridor (500 ft) including grading plan, drainage profile, and utility crossing — recreate a real NYC DOT-style plan set as a portfolio piece

### 🔴 CRITICAL SKILL #2: Site Layout, Grading & Drainage Design
- **Learning Time:** 3-4 months (intermediate technical concept building)
- **Courses:**
  - *Coursera:* "Introduction to Site Planning and Design" (offered via partner universities)
  - *PDH Academy / PE Institute:* "Stormwater Management and Drainage Design" (short CE course, counts toward PDH credits)
- **Books:** "Site Engineering for Landscape Architects" by Steven Strom — surprisingly the best practical grading/drainage reference for civil engineers
- **Hands-On Project:** Take a vacant NYC lot (use public GIS/topo data from NYC Open Data) and produce a full grading + drainage plan, including stormwater calculations
- **Certification Path:** Combine with FE Exam prep (see below) since hydrology/hydraulics is a tested subject area

### 🔴 CRITICAL SKILL #3: Technical Documentation Preparation
- **Learning Time:** 4-6 weeks (fast-track soft/technical hybrid skill)
- **Resources:**
  - *Udemy:* "Technical Writing for Engineers" — short, practical course
  - Study real DOT/DOB plan sets (publicly available via NYC DOT and NYSDOT websites) to learn formatting conventions
- **Hands-On Project:** Convert your Civil 3D grading project (above) into a full technical submission package: cover sheet, notes, specifications, and a one-page engineering memo

### 🔴 CRITICAL SKILL #4: Multi-Disciplinary Team Collaboration
- **Learning Time:** Ongoing, but foundational habits in 4-6 weeks
- **Resources:**
  - *Coursera:* "Teamwork Skills: Communicating Effectively in Groups" (University of Colorado Boulder) — free to audit
  - Join a **student chapter or young professionals group** at ASCE (American Society of Civil Engineers) NYC Metro Section — real collaborative project experience
- **Hands-On Project:** Participate in an ASCE mentorship program or a capstone-style group project (even virtual, via forums like Engineering.com) to practice cross-functional communication

### 🟡 IMPORTANT SKILL #5: Traffic Control / Roadway Design Fundamentals
- **Learning Time:** 2 months
- **Courses:** *PDHengineer.com* — "Work Zone Traffic Control Design" (short course with PDH credit)
- **Resource:** MUTCD (Manual on Uniform Traffic Control Devices) — free federal reference, essential reading
- **Hands-On Project:** Design a temporary traffic control plan for a mock lane closure scenario following MUTCD standards

### 🟡 IMPORTANT SKILL #6: Structural Engineering Fundamentals (for Transit/Systra-track roles)
- **Learning Time:** 3-4 months (builds on undergrad coursework)
- **Courses:** *MIT OpenCourseWare* — "Structural Engineering" (free, self-paced)
- **Certification:** Consider this a **FE Exam prep** overlap area — Structural Analysis is a core FE topic
- **Hands-On Project:** Analyze a simple transit platform beam/column system using free software like SkyCiv (free tier available)

### 🟢 FOUNDATIONAL CERTIFICATION: FE Exam (Fundamentals of Engineering)
- **Priority:** Critical long-term, even though not explicitly listed — this is the industry-standard next step after a BS degree and is implicitly expected given every job's mentorship-to-PE pipeline language
- **Timeline:** 3-4 months of dedicated study
- **Resources:** *School of PE* or *PPI2Pass* FE Civil Review courses; NCEES official practice exams
- **Why:** Passing FE = becoming an EIT (Engineer in Training), a strong signal to employers like Parsons/Systra that you're on the PE track
