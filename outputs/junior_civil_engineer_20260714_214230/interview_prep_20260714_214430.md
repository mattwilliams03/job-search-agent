## Junior Civil Engineer Roles — New York City Market

# SECTION 1: INTERVIEW OVERVIEW

**Number of roles prepared for:** 5 distinct positions across 3 companies (Systra, Actalent, Parsons Corporation)

**Common Interview Formats to Expect:**
- **Screening call** (recruiter, 20-30 min) — especially for Actalent roles since they're a staffing/engineering firm placing candidates with clients
- **Technical phone/video interview** (45-60 min) with hiring manager or senior engineer — expect discussion of coursework, internships, software skills, and design fundamentals
- **In-person or virtual panel interview** — for Systra and Parsons, likely includes a senior engineer, project manager, and possibly HR
- **Portfolio/project review** — bring examples of coursework projects, internship deliverables, or capstone designs (especially AutoCAD Civil 3D work)
- **Possible technical exercise** — a simple grading, drainage, or site layout problem to solve on paper or software during the interview

**General Preparation Timeline (1-2 weeks recommended):**
- **Days 1-3:** Research companies, review job descriptions line-by-line, refresh technical fundamentals (hydraulics, statics, materials, Civil 3D)
- **Days 4-7:** Draft and practice STAR stories for behavioral questions; prepare 2-3 project talking points
- **Days 8-10:** Mock interviews (technical + behavioral), refine answers, prepare questions to ask
- **Days 11-14:** Company-specific deep dive, salary research, final polish, logistics (attire, directions, materials to bring)

## ROLE 1: Jr. Civil/Structural Engineer — Systra

### A. Job Details Recap
- **Company:** Systra | **Salary:** $99,837
- **Key requirements to address:** Rail/mass transit design exposure, structural engineering fundamentals, ability to collaborate with senior engineers on transit infrastructure

### B. Technical Questions

<question>
  <type>Technical</type>
  <text>Walk me through the basic structural design considerations for a rail platform or transit structure. What loads would you need to account for?</text>
  <guidance>
    <evaluating>Foundational structural engineering knowledge and whether you can translate coursework into practical rail/transit applications</evaluating>
    <structure>Start with load types (dead, live, dynamic/impact from trains, seismic if applicable), then discuss material considerations, then safety factors</structure>
    <key_points>
      - Mention dead loads, live loads, dynamic/impact loads from moving trains
      - Reference relevant codes (AREMA for rail, AASHTO, or local NYC building codes)
      - Acknowledge you'd rely on senior PE guidance for final calculations as a junior engineer
    </key_points>
    <example>"In coursework, I learned to calculate dead and live loads, but transit structures also need to account for dynamic loading from train impact and vibration. I'd want to reference AREMA standards and lean on senior engineers to validate my calculations, especially early in my career."</example>
    <avoid>Don't overstate expertise — Systra expects juniors to know fundamentals, not to design independently yet. Avoid pretending you know codes you haven't studied.</avoid>
  </guidance>
</question>

<question>
  <type>Technical</type>
  <text>Have you used any structural analysis software (SAP2000, STAAD.Pro, RISA) or civil design tools like AutoCAD Civil 3D? Describe a project where you applied them.</text>
  <guidance>
    <evaluating>Software proficiency and hands-on application experience</evaluating>
    <structure>Name the software, describe the specific project/assignment, explain the outcome</structure>
    <key_points>
      - Be honest about your exact level of proficiency (beginner/intermediate)
      - Connect software use to a real deliverable (capstone project, internship task)
      - Express eagerness to deepen skills on the job
    </key_points>
    <example>"During my capstone project, I used AutoCAD Civil 3D to design a site grading plan, which taught me contour interpretation and drainage flow paths. I haven't used STAAD.Pro professionally yet, but I'm a fast learner and picked up SAP2000 basics in my structural analysis course."</example>
    <avoid>Avoid vague answers like "I'm familiar with most software" — be specific and honest.</avoid>
  </guidance>
</question>

<question>
  <type>Technical</type>
  <text>What do you know about rail/mass transit infrastructure design that differs from typical building or roadway design?</text>
  <guidance>
    <evaluating>Genuine interest in and research into the transit sector specifically (shows you didn't apply randomly)</evaluating>
    <structure>Compare/contrast transit design considerations vs. general civil work</structure>
    <key_points>
      - Mention clearance envelopes, track alignment, station accessibility (ADA), vibration/noise mitigation
      - Reference Systra's global rail expertise if you researched it
      - Show curiosity, not just textbook knowledge
    </key_points>
    <example>"Transit projects require tight coordination around track geometry, clearance envelopes, and ADA-compliant station access — different from typical site design where you have more flexibility. I know Systra has deep expertise in this from their global rail portfolio, which is part of why I'm excited about this role."</example>
    <avoid>Don't fake deep expertise — it's fine to admit you're early in learning this specialty as long as you show genuine curiosity.</avoid>
  </guidance>
</question>

### C. Behavioral Questions

<question>
  <type>Behavioral</type>
  <text>Tell me about a time you worked closely with someone more experienced than you (a professor, senior engineer, or mentor). How did you handle feedback or correction?</text>
  <guidance>
    <evaluating>Coachability and ability to work under senior engineer mentorship — critical since this is a junior role reporting to PEs</evaluating>
    <structure>Use STAR: Situation, Task, Action, Result</structure>
    <key_points>
      - Show humility and openness to feedback
      - Demonstrate you took corrective action without being defensive
      - End with a positive outcome or lesson learned
    </key_points>
    <example>"Situation: During an internship, my supervisor caught an error in my drainage calculations. Task: I needed to understand my mistake and correct it quickly. Action: I asked clarifying questions, redid the calculation, and asked him to review my revised logic. Result: I not only fixed the error but built his trust — he started giving me more complex tasks afterward."</example>
    <avoid>Don't tell a story where you resisted feedback or where you don't take ownership of a mistake.</avoid>
  </guidance>
</question>

<question>
  <type>Behavioral</type>
  <text>Describe a team project where you had a disagreement with a teammate. How did you resolve it?</text>
  <guidance>
    <evaluating>Conflict resolution and teamwork skills, since engineering projects are highly collaborative</evaluating>
    <structure>STAR method — emphasize communication and compromise</structure>
    <key_points>
      - Focus on professional, respectful resolution
      - Show you can advocate for your ideas while listening to others
      - Highlight the project outcome improved because of the resolution
    </key_points>
    <example>"On my senior design team, a teammate and I disagreed on the drainage layout approach. I proposed we each model our solution and compare cost/performance data objectively rather than debate opinions. We ended up combining the best parts of both designs, which improved our final grade and taught me the value of data-driven compromise."</example>
    <avoid>Avoid blaming the teammate or making yourself look inflexible.</avoid>
  </guidance>
</question>

### D. Situational Questions

<question>
  <type>Situational</type>
  <text>If you were assigned a task with an ambiguous or incomplete set of instructions from a senior engineer who is often busy, how would you proceed?</text>
  <guidance>
    <evaluating>Independence, judgment, and communication skills — important since junior engineers must balance initiative with knowing when to ask for help</evaluating>
    <structure>Describe a logical process: attempt to clarify, do preliminary work, seek help at the right checkpoint</structure>
    <key_points>
      - Show you'd try to answer basic questions yourself first (via specs, codes, precedent projects)
      - Emphasize you'd batch questions efficiently rather than repeatedly interrupting
      - Stress you'd rather ask than guess on anything safety-critical
    </key_points>
    <example>"I'd first review any available project documentation, specs, or similar past projects to fill gaps myself. For anything ambiguous that could affect safety or design intent, I'd compile my questions and request a brief check-in rather than interrupting repeatedly, respecting their time while ensuring accuracy."</example>
    <avoid>Don't say you'd either "just figure it out alone" (risky) or "ask them immediately for everything" (shows lack of initiative).</avoid>
  </guidance>
</question>

### E. Role/Company-Specific Questions

<question>
  <type>Role-specific</type>
  <text>Why are you interested in rail and mass transit infrastructure specifically, and why Systra?</text>
  <guidance>
    <evaluating>Genuine interest vs. generic job-seeking; cultural fit with a specialized global firm</evaluating>
    <structure>Personal motivation + company research + career alignment</structure>
    <key_points>
      - Reference Systra's global reputation (60+ years, 150 countries) and NYC HQ presence
      - Connect to personal interest in public infrastructure/transit's societal impact
      - Mention long-term career growth in a specialized niche
    </key_points>
    <example>"I'm drawn to transit engineering because it has direct daily impact on how millions of people move through cities. Systra's global track record and deep specialization in rail mean I'd be learning from some of the best in the field, and I see this as a strong foundation for building specialized expertise early in my career."</example>
    <avoid>Don't give a generic "I want to grow as an engineer" answer without connecting to transit/rail specifically or Systra's identity.</avoid>
  </guidance>
</question>
